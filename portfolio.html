    Tap-a-beaver

    What I liked

    It started as a Whack-a-mole tutorial exercise, but, looking at the image I found, I thought who could ever 'whack' such a cute little face, plus a friend kindly pointed out to me that that was not a mole...

    What I've learned

    How to play with setIntervals, countDowns and Math.random to actually make the game do what you want it to do and especially stop and reset when the game is over! The beaver had the tendecy to keep popping up totally oblivious of my timer.

    Snake

    What I liked

    Nokia 3310 nostalgia. The idea and the attempt of recreating something similar to the original game has been a quite impressive experience. Something clicked during the realisation of this project and the conversation started to be two-way.

    What I've learned

    The concept of 'thinking like a programmer' and the need to look at the same problem from alternative perspectives over and over again until a solution is found. This has been crucial in understanding move outcomes and implementing two sets of controllers, for keyboard and mobile users.
